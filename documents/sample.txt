# RAG Complete Guide — Sample Document

## What is RAG?
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that enhances Large Language Models
by retrieving relevant context from external knowledge bases before generating a response.

## Why use RAG?
1. Reduces hallucinations by grounding responses in real documents.
2. Enables LLMs to answer questions about private or domain-specific data.
3. Keeps knowledge up to date without retraining the model.

## Core Components
- Document Loaders: Ingest PDFs, HTML, text, CSV, etc.
- Text Splitters: Break documents into manageable chunks.
- Embeddings: Convert text chunks into dense vectors.
- Vector Store: Store and search vectors (e.g., ChromaDB).
- Retriever: Find the most relevant chunks for a query.
- Generator: Feed retrieved context to an LLM to produce the final answer.
